Destinations

These are some of the places I have been able to visit so far, along with my dream destination. I wanted to put them in a table so I can see my journey in one place and show other students that it is possible to travel, even while balancing school, work, and other responsibilities.

Summary of Places I Have Traveled and My Dream Destination
Destination Region Status Highlight Notes
Fiji Country Visited Warm, welcoming people and food that reminded me of the Philippines. [Fiji tourism board] Be open to trying local dishes and talking with locals; bring cash for small markets.
Mexico (Cruise) Country Visited Fresh seafood tacos from La Guerrerense and colorful street life in port. [La Guerrerense] Plan time to explore off the ship and budget for food—you will want to try everything.
Hawaii – Maui, Kauaʻi, Big Island Islands (USA) Visited Beautiful beaches, mountains, and a peaceful island atmosphere. [Go Hawaii] Rent a car if possible and start activities early in the day to avoid crowds and heat.
Hawaii – Oʻahu Island (USA) Living/Studying Studying at BYU–Hawaii and experiencing many cultures in one small place. Take time to learn from classmates and coworkers—they are one of the best “sights” here.
Utah State (USA) Visited Visiting several LDS temples and spending time with friends and members. [Temple list] Weather can change quickly, so pack layers and plan indoor and outdoor activities.
Washington State (USA) Visited Hiking Mt. Rainier and seeing amazing views with friends. [Mt. Rainier National Park] Wear good shoes and check the weather and trail conditions before hiking.
California State (USA) Visited Crossing the Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco and seeing the city. [Golden Gate Bridge] Traffic and parking can be expensive, so use public transportation when you can.
Nevada State (USA) Visited Seeing my first snow at Mt. Rose during springtime. Bring warm clothing even in spring; mountain areas can still be very cold.
Europe Continent Dream Destination Exploring historic cities, museums, temples, and trying different European foods. I know I will need to plan ahead, save more, and research transportation between countries.

Most of the places I have visited so far are around the Pacific—different islands and states that still feel connected by the ocean. Europe is my “stretch” dream, something bigger that I am working toward as I continue my studies and career. Looking at this table reminds me of how far I have already come and how much I have learned from each place. It also motivates me to keep exploring the world in a smart, budget-conscious way while I am still young.
