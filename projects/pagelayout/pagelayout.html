What is good practice?

A good hula session starts small and stays consistent. I set a quick goal, like ten clean kaholo steps or a smooth hand pattern. When I keep the reps even and breathe, my timing feels better and the moves look cleaner.

I also write one note after I finish. It can be as simple as “slow down the hands” or “bend knees more.” Next time I read that note before I start. This keeps me improving a little each day without feeling overwhelmed.
